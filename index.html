1 Know Your Data: Variable Types

Before drawing any chart, you must know what type of data you have. In statistics, variables are mainly divided into two large families:

Qualitative (Categorical)

They describe qualities, characteristics, or categories. They are not mathematically operable values.

  • Nominal: They have no logical order.
    Ex: Colors, Countries, Brands.
  • Ordinal: They have a natural order or hierarchy.
    Ex: Sizes (S, M, L), Education level.

Quantitative (Numerical)

They are real numbers. They represent quantities or measurements that can be mathematically operated.

  • Discrete: Whole numbers (result of counting).
    Ex: Number of children, amount of cars.
  • Continuous: Can have decimals (result of measuring).
    Ex: Height, weight, salary.
Note on Time: Although time (Dates, Hours) is a continuous variable, in data visualization it is treated as a special category (Temporal) because it almost exclusively dictates the use of line or area charts to show evolution over time.
